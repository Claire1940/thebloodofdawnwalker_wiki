## Why the exact date is still missing in 2026

For big narrative RPGs, a “year first, date later” strategy is common. Studios usually announce the exact date after they hit internal milestones tied to QA, localization, certification, and marketing commitments.

Here’s the practical reason: giving a date too early increases delay risk, which can hurt momentum and trust.

| Pre-Launch Milestone | Why It Matters | Typical Impact on Date Reveal |
|---|---|---|
| **Feature lock** | Core systems stop changing heavily | Date can be announced more safely |
| **Performance pass** | Frame pacing and stability targets | Reduces risk of late delays |
| **Localization finalization** | Multi-region launch readiness | Needed for global date confidence |
| **Platform certification prep** | Console technical compliance | Often gates final release timing |
| **Marketing asset lock** | Trailers, store pages, ad buys | Usually starts when date is firm |

In short, no fixed day yet does **not** mean trouble by default. It often means the team is avoiding a premature commitment.

> **Tip:** Watch for a burst of synchronized marketing (new trailer + storefront update + pre-order details). That combination often comes right before an exact-date announcement.
