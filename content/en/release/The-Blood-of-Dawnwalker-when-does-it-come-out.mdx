## Should you buy at launch? Key gameplay systems that affect the decision

The release date matters, but your *fit* with the game’s systems matters more. Dawnwalker is positioned as a narrative-heavy action RPG with strong consequence design.

### 1) Day/night identity and transformation
You play with dual-state mechanics tied to time of day, including vampiric abilities at night and different daytime interactions. There is also a wolf transformation perk that appears to function as mobility utility.

### 2) Corruption and risk management
Feeding increases corruption. Higher corruption can unlock stronger vampiric options but may create loss-of-control outcomes tied to narrative consequences.

### 3) Infamy and world response
Aggressive choices can raise infamy, which can make the world more hostile and alter quest dynamics. This is not just “morality flavor”; it appears to affect challenge and story routes.

### 4) Time-linked progression
Progression appears tied to an in-world timeline where actions can consume time. If you prefer fully open-ended grinding before plot pressure, this may feel restrictive.
