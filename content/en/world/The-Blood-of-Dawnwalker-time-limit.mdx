## How to Plan Around the Blood of Dawnwalker Time Limit

If you want to enjoy the game without constant anxiety, use a layered planning approach: scout, prioritize, commit, and adapt.

### 1) Scout first, commit second
Before locking into long quest chains, gather map intel and identify clusters of related objectives.

### 2) Rank quests by strategic value
Not every quest should get equal weight under a timed structure. Divide tasks by impact.

| Priority Tier | Quest Traits | Recommended Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1: Critical** | Family rescue leads, faction unlocks, major power shifts | Do early |
| **Tier 2: Build-Up** | Gear upgrades, ally recruitment, traversal tools | Fit between critical arcs |
| **Tier 3: Flavor/Optional** | Lore-only tasks, low-impact errands | Save for surplus time |
| **Tier 4: Risky Detours** | Long travel + unclear reward | Delay unless needed |

### 3) Budget your “decision days”
Think in blocks. If a major branch likely consumes multiple clock ticks, leave room for fallout quests and contingency objectives.

### 4) Keep one flexible slot in your plan
Reactive world design means opportunities can emerge suddenly. Avoid over-scheduling every segment of your run.

> **💡 Tip:** In timed RPG structures, “efficient” and “rushed” are different. Efficient means selecting high-value actions; rushed means acting without information.
